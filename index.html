Background

A premier platform for responsible mining investment in Kenya's frontier county.

Brought together by a shared commitment to advancing responsible artisanal and small-scale mining (ASM) in Kenya, Solidaridad, the Kenya Oil and Gas Working Group (KOGWG), Geomine Consulting Limited, the Mining Engineers Society of Kenya (MESK), the Turkana County Government, the Kenya Chamber of Mines, and the State Department of Mining proudly present the Turkana Gold Summit & Minerals Expo 2026.

The Summit will serve as Turkana County’s premier mining investment, policy, and stakeholder engagement platform, bringing together government leaders, miners, investors, financial institutions, equipment suppliers, development partners, and industry experts to unlock the county’s growing mineral potential.

Positioned as a strategic conference, exhibition, and networking forum, the event aims to position Turkana as a destination for responsible mining investment while promoting sustainable livelihoods, access to finance, modern technology, and stronger governance within the ASM sector.

Why Attend

What you gain by being in the room.

Turkana County is rapidly emerging as one of Kenya’s most resource-rich regions, with growing artisanal gold mining activities complementing its petroleum, renewable energy, and other mineral resources. Gold mining has become an important source of income for many communities, particularly youth and women, yet the sector remains largely informal, underfinanced, and disconnected from responsible markets. At the same time, investors, financial institutions, and equipment suppliers require a predictable and well- governed environment before committing resources. Summit Objectives & Theme

Shaping Responsible Mining in Turkana.

Six strategic themes anchor every session, exhibition and site visit.

The Turkana Gold Summit & Minerals Expo 2026 is designed as a transformative, action-oriented convening that will bring together over 300 delegates from across the entire mining value chain. Participants will include senior representatives from government regulatory agencies, artisanal and small-scale mining associations, cooperatives, commercial banks and microfinance institutions, established mining companies, equipment manufacturers and technology providers, impact investors and development finance institutions, academic researchers, and civil society organizations committed to responsible resource governance.

Over the course of three immersive days, delegates will gain practical, evidence-based insights into responsible mining practices that balance productivity with environmental stewardship. The programme is structured to unlock concrete financing opportunities by connecting miners and cooperatives directly with banks, SACCOs, and impact investors actively seeking to deploy capital in the ASM sector. Attendees will receive detailed briefings on the latest regulatory developments ; including reforms to the Mining Act, the formalization roadmap for artisanal operators, and compliance frameworks governing mercury-free processing , while exploring emerging technologies in mineral processing, digital traceability systems, and occupational health and safety solutions. Above all, the Summit is a dedicated relationship-building platform where strategic partnerships are forged between government, private sector, and development actors , partnerships that will accelerate the growth, formalization, and long-term sustainability of Turkana's mining economy.
